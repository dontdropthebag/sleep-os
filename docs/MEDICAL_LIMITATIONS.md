# Medical limitations

Read this before trusting any number in the app.

1. **Consumer sleep trackers are not polysomnography.** Phone accelerometers and wearables
   estimate sleep from movement, heart rate and sound. They are useful for trends, not for
   diagnosis.
2. **Sleep-stage estimates can be substantially inaccurate.** Deep/REM/light minutes are
   device estimates and are labelled as such everywhere in this app. One "bad deep sleep"
   night means very little.
3. **HRV varies enormously between individuals.** Only your own trend, measured the same
   way by the same device, is meaningful. A low reading is context (alcohol, illness,
   training, travel) — not a health verdict.
4. **Snoring detection cannot confirm or exclude sleep apnea.** A phone microphone or
   wearable is not a diagnostic tool. If snoring occurs together with witnessed breathing
   pauses, gasping or choking at night, significant daytime sleepiness, repeated morning
   headaches, or waking with a racing heart, discuss a sleep assessment with a healthcare
   professional. The app shows this suggestion calmly and only when such combinations
   appear — it never diagnoses.
5. **This application is educational and observational.** It identifies patterns and
   supports personal experiments. It does not diagnose insomnia, sleep apnea,
   cardiovascular disease or any other condition, and its "readiness" outputs are
   system-derived estimates, not medical recovery measurements.
6. **Persistent problems belong with a professional.** Ongoing insomnia, loud nightly
   snoring, dangerous sleepiness while driving, or anything that worries you is a reason to
   talk to a qualified clinician. Use the *Export for clinician* function to bring useful
   data (with its limitations stated) to that conversation.
7. **Orthosomnia is real.** Obsessing over nightly scores can itself harm sleep. The app
   avoids alarmist language, prioritises weekly trends over single nights, and offers a
   setting to hide nightly scores entirely (Settings → Wellbeing guard).
